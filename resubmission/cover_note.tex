\noindent Dear Editors,

\bigskip

\noindent We thank the Editors for the opportunity to transfer our manuscript,
\textit{``How Focused Are LLMs? A Quantitative Study via Repetitive Deterministic
Prediction Tasks''} (manuscript XQ11175), to \textit{Physical Review Research}, and we
are grateful to both Referees for their careful and constructive reports. We have revised
the manuscript to address every point raised. No new experiments were required: all
strengthening derives from re-analysis of the already-collected data and from clearer
framing.

\medskip

\noindent The principal changes are:
\begin{itemize}
  \item every accuracy-versus-length curve now reports explicit sample sizes (up to
        $n=100$ instances per length) with $95\%$ confidence intervals;
  \item the Sherrington--Kirkpatrick mapping is now presented as an \emph{effective,
        phenomenological} model that reproduces the observed crossover and yields a compact
        two-parameter diagnostic, with earlier first-principles claims about attention removed;
  \item we reconcile the perturbative expansion and the double-exponential law, correcting a
        coefficient in the large-field reduction and stating explicitly that the closed form is
        a phenomenological approximation valid for small $J_0$ and large $h$, not a strict
        term-by-term resummation; and
  \item we add a scaling-collapse figure that directly tests and confirms the universal
        finite-size crossover predicted by the model.
\end{itemize}

\noindent A point-by-point response to both Referees and an itemized list of changes accompany
this resubmission, together with a change-marked version of the manuscript.

\medskip

\noindent We believe the revised manuscript is well matched to the scope and criteria of
\textit{Physical Review Research}, and we thank the Editors and Referees again for their time.

\bigskip

\noindent Sincerely,\\
The Authors
